Part 1 — C1 Awareness (10 min)

5 min: Play podcast. Students listen only.
5 min: Fast group discussion — 4 questions, 2–3 students each.

While Listening (5 min) 0–5 min

Tell students before pressing play: "Just listen. Notice how the speaker sounds — not only what they say."

1
How fast is the speaker talking? Can you keep up?
2
Notice the vocabulary — are there words you do not know? How many?
3
How does the speaker connect ideas? Listen for linking words and phrases.
C1 Awareness Discussion (5 min) 5–10 min

Fast rotation — 2–3 students per question. Do not linger.

1
What does C1 English sound like? Name 3 specific things you noticed in the podcast.
Push for:
Speaks fast
Uses many different words
Long sentences
Does not stop or pause
Sounds confident
Uses linking words
States opinion clearly
Pauses in the right place
Uses complex grammar
Voice goes up and down
Does not say "um" or "uh"
Chooses exact words
2
How much of the podcast did you understand — 40%? 70%? 90%? What caused the gaps?
Push for:
Speaker was too fast
Did not know the words
Ideas were too hard
Accent was hard to follow
Did not know the topic
Sentences were too long
No time to catch up
Grammar was too complex
3
What is the difference between B2 and C1 English? Describe it without using "more" or "better."
Push for:
Uses exact words
Wider vocabulary
Sounds natural
Does not stop under pressure
Can explain complex ideas
Connects ideas smoothly
Complex grammar
e.g. passive voice, conditionals, reported speech
Complex sentence structure
e.g. Although..., Not only... but also..., Despite...
4
What is the ONE thing that still feels hardest for you at C1? Be specific.
Options to offer:
Finding the right words fast
Speaking without stopping
Pausing too much
Listening for a long time
Feeling confident
Using complex grammar

Final Reflection — 5 min · Whole Class
"Which idea from Chapter 1 is the most useful for life, study, or work? Why?"
Go around the room. One sentence each. No repeating what someone else said.

Speaking Frame — All Students

Write this on the board. Every student uses this structure for every answer.

Universal Speaking Structure
1
I think that...
State your opinion clearly in the first sentence. Do not wait.
2
The first reason is that...
Give your stronger reason first. Be specific.
3
The second reason is that...
Support with a second point. Do not repeat the first.
4
In the chapter, Collins says that...
Quote or reference the book directly.
5
For example...
Use a real example from life, school, work, or Mongolia.
6
However, some people may say that...
Show you can see the other side. This is what C1 sounds like.
7
So, in my opinion...
Close with your final position. Clear and confident.
Linking Words — Use these to connect your ideas
Purpose Linking Word Example
To show contrast However, "However, some people disagree."
To show contrast Although "Although this is true, I think..."
To show contrast On the other hand, "On the other hand, Collins argues..."
To add a point Furthermore, "Furthermore, Collins shows that..."
To add a point In addition, "In addition, the research shows..."
To add a point Moreover, "Moreover, this idea applies to..."
To give an example For instance, "For instance, in Mongolia..."
To give an example For example, "For example, a company like..."
To show consequence As a result, "As a result, the company grew."
To show consequence Therefore, "Therefore, I believe that..."
To show consequence Consequently, "Consequently, great companies..."
To conclude In conclusion, "In conclusion, I think Collins is..."
To conclude Overall, "Overall, this idea is important because..."

SOS — If Class Gets Stuck

Quick fixes for common classroom problems.

Student goes silent

Ask: "What do you think Collins would say about this?" — redirects pressure away from personal opinion.

Answer is too short

Use: "Can you give an example?" or "Why do you think that?" — never accept one sentence at C1.

Running out of time

Skip S11 and S12. Move directly to Final Reflection — still gets closure.

Great discussion, want more

Ask: "Does anyone disagree with what [student] just said?" — instant debate.

Student did not read

Give them S3 or S10 — very clear book quotes they can build on without full reading.

Discussion dries up

Pair talk 2 min: "Tell your partner — is Collins right?" Then bring back to group.
