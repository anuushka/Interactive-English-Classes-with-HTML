Debate Algorithm — Step by Step
1
Assign sides — don't ask
Split roster down the middle. Students defend their assigned position whether they agree or not.
↓
2
Side A makes their case — 2 min
Call a specific name. Don't wait for volunteers. If they pause: "What's your main argument?"
↓
3
Side B responds directly — 2 min
Must react to what Side A actually said — not give a new argument. "[Name] — do you agree with that?"
↓
4
Open floor — use the Chain Method
Stop being the hub. Students talk to each other. See Chain Method below.
↓
5
Energy drops — drop a trap question
See SOS tab. One sharp question always restarts the room.
The Chain Method

Students talk to each other — not to you. Pass the conversation like a chain.

Teacher
calls →
Student A
passes to →
Student B
passes to →
Student C
→ next unheard
→
After each speaker: "[Name] — do you agree with what [Name] just said?"
→
After a few turns: "[Name] — who do you want to respond?" — students start picking each other
Chain Method — One Rule

Never return to the same student twice until every student has spoken once.

Teacher Rules During Output
✗
Never correct mid-speech. Let errors go during output.
✓
Mirror instead of fix. Repeat their idea back with the correct form naturally.
✗
Never ask the open room. "Does anyone want to share?" = silence. Always call a name.
✓
React to content, not language. Students feel heard → filter drops → more output.
✗
Never let one student dominate. After 60 sec: "Strong — [new name], what do you think?"
✓
Plant grammar in your own speech. Students acquire by hearing, not by being told.
Silence — Decision Tree
Room goes quiet
Nobody has spoken yet
Call the most confident student. Ask a yes/no question first.
Same 2–3 people talking
Stop the chain. Call a quiet student: "[Name] — we haven't heard from you."
General energy drop
Chat pulse — YES/NO into the chat. Then call someone who typed.
Topic exhausted
Drop a trap question from the SOS tab.
