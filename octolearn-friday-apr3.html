OUTPUT · 0:49–1:25
Debate: People vs Strategy
⏱ 36 min — Assign sides, chain method, Flywheel injection mid-debate
Side A — Collins' view

"Get the right people on the bus first. Strategy will follow. The who always matters more than the what."

Side B — Challenge

"Without a clear strategy, even great people waste their talent. Direction has to come first."

How to run it
1

Assign sides. Split roster. No choosing.

2

Side A: 2 min — call specific names.

3

Side B: 2 min — direct response to what Side A said.

4

Open floor: Chain Method. Every student speaks before time ends.

Trap question — inject mid-debate

"Collins says you can have the right people and the wrong strategy and still succeed — because the right people will figure it out. But can you have the right strategy with the wrong people? What happens then?"

Follow-up prompts — use in order if needed
1

"Think of OctoLearn — or your own workplace. Is the main challenge finding the right strategy, or finding the right people?"

2

"If you were building a team from scratch tomorrow, what's the first thing you'd decide — the mission or the people?"

3

"Collins says great companies got the right people on the bus before they knew where the bus was going. Does that make sense — or does it sound like chaos?"

Flywheel vs Doom Loop — inject in the final 8 min

Collins says the Doom Loop is what happens when companies keep lurching from strategy to strategy — each new leader brings a new plan, momentum never builds. The Flywheel is the opposite: consistent push in one direction until results compound.


"Is the Doom Loop a people problem or a strategy problem? Which one causes the other?"

Flywheel vs Doom Loop — remind students
FlywheelDoom Loop
Consistent push, one directionConstant change, chasing trends
Momentum builds slowly, then compoundsLurching from idea to idea
Discipline over timeReactive, no sustained effort
Example answers
"I recognize the Doom Loop in my company — every year there's a new strategy, a new direction. Nothing ever compounds. If we had stayed consistent, we would have grown by now."
"Honestly, my own learning has been a Doom Loop. I've been switching between English apps, courses, methods — no momentum. If I had stayed with one method, I'd be much further."
"OctoLearn feels like a Flywheel to me — consistent classes, consistent method. Results are starting to compound."
How to run it
1

Fixed order — alphabetical or screen order. Everyone knows their turn is coming.

2

30 seconds each. This is personal — let it breathe a little more than usual.

3

Teacher responds with one word: "Honest." / "Real." / "Strong." / "Exactly."

4

No correction. No follow-up. The conditionals will appear naturally.
